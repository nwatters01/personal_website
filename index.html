Bio

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at MIT. My current research focuses on studying cognitive science questions using tools from systems neuroscience, though going forward I am interested in transitioning to work on brain computer interfaces. My background is in AI & machine learning, so I'm particularly interested in questions that bear on shortcomings of current AI (e.g. how the brain does transfer learning, generalization, compositional planning, etc.). Currently, I work in Mehrdad Jazayeri's lab doing neurophysiology on monkeys playing video games, focusing on neural representations of objects and dynamics.

Aside from research, I like playing music (jazz piano), sports (ultimate frisbee, soccer, climbing), and board games.
